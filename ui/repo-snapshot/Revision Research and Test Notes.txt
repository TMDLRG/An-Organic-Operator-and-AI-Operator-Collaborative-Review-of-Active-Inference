## 1. Executive Verdict

**Overall mathematical soundness:** *Core algebra correct; interpretive layer unsound; bridge to CVM unproved.*

The central target identity in SOURCE A's Eqn. 2,
$$
F(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r) \;=\; \mathbb E_q[L(\tilde x)] - H[q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)] \;=\; L(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\left[q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)\,\|\,p(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r)\right],
$$
is the standard variational identity, recoverable from Beal (2003) Eqns. 2.12–2.16 and identical in content to SOURCE B Eqn. 2.5 ([book line 1299](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:1299)). The pedagogical intent — to bridge Beal, Friston, and Blei notations — is valuable and partially successful.

However, the manuscript is **not yet publication-ready as a rigorous derivation paper**. Six issues are serious:

1. **q/p role reversal** in the parenthetical note under Eqn. 2 ([SOURCE A:34](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:34)) inverts the standard VI assignment.
2. **Bound direction reversed** in Section 6.2 ([SOURCE A:315–319](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:315)). SOURCE B states the correct direction five times ([lines 1397, 1452, 1605, 2284, 2433](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:1397)): "free energy is an upper bound on surprise."
3. **Posterior conflated with external process** ([SOURCE A:132](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:132)) — calls $p(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r)$ "the actual distribution of the external system itself." SOURCE B is explicit ([line 1099](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:1099)): the hidden states of the generative model and the generative process are *not the same*.
4. **L(s,a,r) expansion** ([SOURCE A:166](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:166)) writes $L = -\sum_{i=1}^I \ln p(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r)$ as a sum of $I$ identical terms, which is dimensionally incoherent without an iid factorization explicitly stated.
5. **Integration substitution** ([SOURCE A:105–106](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:105)) reinterprets $\int \cdot\, d\tilde\eta$ as integration over $\tilde r$ without a pushforward map, induced measure, or Jacobian.

A seventh issue is moderate but persistent: **Appendix B Eqn. B-1** ([SOURCE A:457](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:457)) writes the energy-like term as $H = \sum_i \int dx_i\, q(x_i) \ln p(x_i,y_i\mid\theta)$ — missing the negative sign. As written it is the negative of expected energy, not expected energy.

## 2. Grounded Truth Summary

This is the corrected core, from first principles. The standard VI identity and the corrected role assignments are:

**Setup.** Let $y$ denote observed variables. In Friston/active-inference notation $y=(\tilde s, \tilde a, \tilde r)$ where $\tilde s$ are sensory states, $\tilde a$ active states, $\tilde r$ internal/representational states. Let $\tilde\eta$ denote external/hidden states. Let $p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)$ denote the agent's *generative model* (a joint density indexed by model parameters $m$). Let $q(\tilde\eta\mid \tilde r)$ denote the *variational density* (sometimes "recognition density"), parameterized by the internal states $\tilde r$.

**Master identity (proved in §6 below).** Under the assumptions $p(y\mid m) > 0$, common support of $q$ and $p(\cdot\mid y, m)$, and integrability of all log terms,
$$
\boxed{\;F[q] \;:=\; \mathbb E_q\!\left[-\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)\right] - H[q]
\;=\; \underbrace{-\ln p(y\mid m)}_{=\,L(y),\ \text{surprisal}} \;+\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)\,\|\,p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)\bigr).\;}
$$

**Corrected role assignment.**
- $q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)$ = approximate / variational posterior. *Not the true posterior.*
- $p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)$ = exact posterior under the generative model. *Not the external system itself.*
- The model posterior $p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)$ is a quantity inside the agent's model. It is conceptually distinct from the actual external generative process, which the model only approximates.

**Correct bound direction.**
- $D_{\mathrm{KL}} \ge 0 \Rightarrow F[q] \ge -\ln p(y\mid m) = L(y)$. **Friston-style VFE is an UPPER bound on surprisal / negative log evidence.**
- Equivalently, the ELBO $\mathcal L(q) := \mathbb E_q[\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)] + H[q] = \ln p(y\mid m) - D_{\mathrm{KL}} \le \ln p(y\mid m)$. **ELBO is a LOWER bound on log evidence.**
- $F[q] = -\mathcal L(q)$.

**Correct status of CVM bridge.** The CVM is a *separate variational principle* on a Markov random field with a Kikuchi cluster-entropy approximation. It has not been shown in SOURCE A to be either equal to, or a principled approximation of, the active-inference free-energy functional $F[q]$. Section 8 is a research proposal of value, but it is not a theorem.

## 6. Corrected Core Derivation

Below is a publication-grade, first-principles derivation of the master identity. This is the form Section 2 of SOURCE A should converge to.

### 6.1 Definitions and assumptions

**Definitions.**
1. Generative model: $p(y, \tilde\eta \mid m)$, a joint probability density (or mass function) over observed variables $y$ and hidden/external variables $\tilde\eta$, indexed by model parameters $m$. In the Friston/Maren active-inference setting, $y = (\tilde s, \tilde a, \tilde r)$.
2. Marginal model evidence: $p(y \mid m) := \int p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)\, d\tilde\eta$.
3. Exact posterior under the generative model: $p(\tilde\eta \mid y, m) := p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m) / p(y\mid m)$, defined wherever $p(y\mid m) > 0$.
4. Variational density (recognition density): $q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)$, an arbitrary density over $\tilde\eta$ that may be parameterized by internal states $\tilde r$ and/or sufficient statistics $\mu$. Member of a chosen family $\mathcal Q$.
5. Surprisal / negative log evidence: $L(y) := -\ln p(y\mid m)$.
6. Shannon entropy of $q$: $H[q] := -\int q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r) \ln q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)\, d\tilde\eta$.
7. Kullback–Leibler divergence (forward to $p$): $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q \| p) := \int q \ln(q/p)$, defined whenever the integrand is well-defined and $q \ll p$ (q is absolutely continuous wrt p).

**Assumptions.**
- A1 (positivity of evidence): $p(y\mid m) > 0$.
- A2 (common support): $q \ll p(\cdot \mid y, m)$ (so the KL is finite).
- A3 (integrability): $\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)$ and $\ln q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)$ are $q$-integrable.

These three suffice for everything below. Stochastic independence between $q$ and $p$ is *not* required and *not* meaningful as stated in [SOURCE A:285](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:285).

### 6.2 The master identity

**Definition of variational free energy.** Set
$$
F[q] \;:=\; \int q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r) \ln \frac{q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)}{p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)}\, d\tilde\eta.
$$

**Form 1 (energy minus entropy).** Splitting the logarithm,
$$
F[q] = \int q\,\ln q\, d\tilde\eta \;-\; \int q\,\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)\, d\tilde\eta = -H[q] + \mathbb E_q\!\left[-\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)\right].
$$
Rearranging:
$$
\boxed{\;F[q] = \mathbb E_q\!\left[-\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)\right] - H[q].\;} \tag{$\star$}
$$
The first term is "expected energy" or "expected negative log joint." It is *structurally* analogous to thermodynamic energy but is not a physical energy.

**Form 2 (surprisal plus KL divergence).** Apply Bayes' rule (Assumption A1):
$$
\ln p(y, \tilde\eta\mid m) = \ln p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m) + \ln p(y\mid m).
$$
Substitute into the definition of $F[q]$:
$$
F[q] = \int q \ln \frac{q}{p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)\, p(y\mid m)}\, d\tilde\eta = \int q \ln \frac{q}{p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)}\, d\tilde\eta \;-\; \ln p(y\mid m) \int q\, d\tilde\eta.
$$
Using $\int q\, d\tilde\eta = 1$:
$$
\boxed{\;F[q] = D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r) \,\big\|\, p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)\bigr) \;-\; \ln p(y\mid m) \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\|p(\tilde\eta\mid y)) + L(y).\;} \tag{$\star\star$}
$$

Identities $(\star)$ and $(\star\star)$ together are the corrected version of SOURCE A's Eqn. 2.

### 6.3 Bound direction (Lemma)

**Lemma 1.** Under A1–A3, $F[q] \ge L(y) = -\ln p(y\mid m)$, with equality iff $q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r) = p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)$ a.e.

*Proof.* By Gibbs' inequality, $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\|p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)) \ge 0$, with equality iff $q = p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)$. Apply to $(\star\star)$. $\square$

### 6.4 ELBO and sign-convention bridge (Lemma)

**Definition (ELBO).** $\mathcal L(q) := \mathbb E_q[\ln p(y, \tilde\eta\mid m)] + H[q]$.

**Lemma 2 (Beal–Friston sign convention).** $\mathcal L(q) = \ln p(y\mid m) - D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\|p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m))$, hence $\mathcal L(q) \le \ln p(y\mid m)$. Furthermore $F[q] = -\mathcal L(q)$.

*Proof.* From Form 1, $F[q] = -\mathbb E_q[\ln p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)] - H[q] = -\mathcal L(q)$. Combining with $(\star\star)$ gives the displayed identity for $\mathcal L$. The bound follows from KL $\ge 0$. $\square$

**Sign-convention crosswalk.**
- Beal/Blei convention: maximize ELBO $\mathcal L(q)$, which lower-bounds $\ln p(y\mid m)$.
- Friston convention: minimize VFE $F[q]$, which upper-bounds $-\ln p(y\mid m)$.
- These are *equivalent* under sign reversal: $F[q] = -\mathcal L(q)$.

This corrects SOURCE A Section 6.2 and clarifies the two conventions definitively.

## 8. Bound and Sign-Convention Audit

### 8.1 The hierarchy

Under Assumptions A1–A3 of §6:

| Convention | Functional | Bound | Direction |
|------------|------------|-------|-----------|
| ELBO (Beal/Blei/ML) | $\mathcal L(q) = \mathbb E_q[\ln p] + H[q]$ | $\mathcal L(q) \le \ln p(y\mid m)$ | LOWER bound on log evidence |
| VFE (Friston/active inf.) | $F[q] = \mathbb E_q[-\ln p] - H[q]$ | $F[q] \ge -\ln p(y\mid m)$ | UPPER bound on surprisal / negative log evidence |
| Identity | $F[q] = -\mathcal L(q)$ | – | sign flip |
| Tightness | KL $= 0$ | $F[q] = -\ln p(y)$, $\mathcal L(q) = \ln p(y)$ | iff $q = p(\eta\mid y, m)$ |

### 8.2 Why SOURCE A Section 6.2 currently fails

[SOURCE A:319](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:319) reads: *"the free energy for the model is a lower bound for the free energy of the external system. As the model is brought closer to alignment with the external system… the free energy of the model approaches that of the external system $(L(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r) \to F(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r))$."*

Decomposition of the error:
1. The phrase "free energy of the external system" is undefined. In Maren's notation $L = -\ln p$ is *surprisal*, not a free energy of a physical system. Calling it "the free energy of the external system" misnames it.
2. The bound goes the *opposite* direction. From $F[q] = L + D_{\mathrm{KL}} \ge L$ (with $D_{\mathrm{KL}} \ge 0$), $F$ upper-bounds $L$. Hence "$F$ is a lower bound for $L$" reverses the inequality.
3. The arrow $L \to F$ is also inverted. As alignment improves (KL decreases), it is $F$ that approaches $L$ from above, not $L$ that approaches $F$ from below.

### 8.3 Recommended replacement wording for Section 6.2

> "By Gibbs' inequality, $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\|p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)) \ge 0$. From the second form of Eqn. 2, $F(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r) = L(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r) + D_{\mathrm{KL}} \ge L(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r)$, where $L(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r) = -\ln p(\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r\mid m)$ is the surprisal (negative log evidence) of the observed blanket and internal states under the generative model. Thus the variational free energy is an *upper bound* on surprisal. Equivalently (by sign reversal), the negative free energy — the ELBO — is a *lower bound* on the log evidence. As the variational density $q$ is brought closer to the exact posterior $p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)$, the divergence shrinks and $F \to L$ from above."

### 8.4 Stress-test verification (numerical)

§12 Test 1 confirms: with $p(\eta=1) = 0.7$, $p(y=1\mid\eta=1) = 0.9$, $p(y=1\mid\eta=0) = 0.2$, observed $y=1$:
- Surprisal $-\ln p(y=1) = 0.371$ nats, exact posterior $p(\eta=1\mid y=1) = 0.913$.
- For uniform $q$: $F[q] = 0.945 > 0.371$, with margin = KL = 0.574. ✓
- For $q$ = exact posterior: $F[q] = 0.371 = $ surprisal. ✓
- For $q(\eta=1) = 0.95$ (close): $F = 0.381 > 0.371$, margin = KL = 0.010. ✓

## 9. Markov Blanket & Active-Inference Audit

### 9.1 What works in SOURCE A

The manuscript correctly emphasizes that Friston's notation introduces a partition of the system into external states $\tilde\eta$, internal/representational states $\tilde r$, and blanket states $\tilde s, \tilde a$ — a real conceptual departure from Beal's simpler latent-variable VI. Figure 1 and Figure 3 are essentially correct schematic diagrams of the architecture.

### 9.2 What needs tightening

Three distinct things are referred to as "the Markov blanket" and need to be separated:

**(a) Conditional-independence statement.** Per SOURCE B Box 3.1 ([book lines 1932–1948](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:1932)):
$$
\mu \perp\!\!\!\perp x \mid b \iff p(\mu, x \mid b) = p(\mu \mid b)\, p(x \mid b).
$$
This is the formal definition. Numerical verification in §12 Test 8 confirms the factorization on a simple constructed joint.

**(b) Causal / process diagram.** Influence flows in a particular pattern, e.g., "internal states cannot directly change external states but can do so vicariously by changing active states" ([SOURCE B:1961](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:1961)). This is a *dynamic* claim about Jacobians and is logically distinct from (a).

**(c) Inference architecture.** Internal states *parameterize* a recognition density $q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)$. This is a *modeling* choice on top of (a) and (b); the agent uses the blanket structure to organize its inference.

[SOURCE A:23–27, 137–139](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:23) blends these three. The revised manuscript should treat each in turn and label which one it is invoking.

### 9.3 Generative model vs. generative process

SOURCE A repeatedly conflates the model posterior $p(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r)$ with the actual external process. Most explicit at [SOURCE A:132](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:132): *"the actual distribution of the external system itself $p(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde s,\tilde a,\tilde r)$."*

SOURCE B Figure 2.2 caption ([book lines 1094–1106](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:1094)) is the gold standard:
> "The hidden states of the generative model and the generative process are not the same. The organism's model includes a range of hypotheses ($x$) about the hidden state, which do not necessarily include the true value of the hidden state $x^*$ of the generative process… It might be that the hidden states in the external world take on values that lie outside the space of explanations available to the brain."

SOURCE A's collapse of the two is a category error, not a notational shortcut.

### 9.4 "Both systems separately come to free-energy minima"

[SOURCE A:335–341](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:335) and §8.1 generally claim that the external system and the representational system *each separately* achieve free-energy minimization. As a conceptual picture aligned with NESS / active-inference scaffolding (cf. [book lines 2581–2603](book_9780262369978%20(1).txt:2581)), this is defensible. As a derived claim in a VB derivation paper, it is under-argued. The standard VI identity establishes only that an *agent's* internal states minimize $F[q]$; the additional claim that the external generative process is *itself* governed by a free-energy minimum requires a NESS/Helmholtz-machine argument that the manuscript does not provide.

The fix is conservative wording: "Under steady-state assumptions of the type discussed in Friston (2013), the external system can also be characterized by a free-energy-like steady-state distribution. We do not derive that result here; we rely on it as a modeling assumption."

## 10. Thermodynamic Analogy Audit

### 10.1 Where the analogy is valid

The structural form
$$
F[q] = (\text{expected energy}) - (\text{entropy})
$$
is genuinely analogous to the Helmholtz free energy $F = U - TS$ (with the temperature absorbed by reduction). SOURCE A is justified in using this analogy as didactic motivation, especially in §2 and §6.1.

### 10.2 Where the manuscript itself is correct in flagging limits

[SOURCE A:68–73](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:68) usefully quotes Sengupta, Stemmler, & Friston (2013): "*variational free energy is not the Helmholtz free energy* … it is a functional of a probability distribution over hidden (fictive) states encoded by internal states $q(y\mid m)$, not the probability distribution over the (physical) internal states." This is exactly the right warning, and the manuscript deserves credit for including it.

### 10.3 Where the manuscript still overreaches

Three issues remain after the Sengupta caveat:

**(a) "Enthalpy" label.** Calling $\mathbb E_q[-\ln p]$ "enthalpy" is too aggressive. Enthalpy in classical thermodynamics is $H = U + pV$ — internal energy plus pressure-volume work. The variational quantity has no $pV$ analog. Safer terminology: *expected energy*, *expected negative log joint*, or *Gibbs/expected energy term* (with the qualifier).

**(b) Helmholtz vs. Gibbs collapse.** [SOURCE A:70](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:70) Author's Note 2: *"The distinction between Helmholtz and Gibbs free energies disappears when we use the thermodynamic free energy formulations as a metaphor."* This is rhetorically understandable but mathematically risky. Helmholtz $A = U - TS$ and Gibbs $G = H - TS = U + pV - TS$ are *different* state functions. Under the metaphor, neither is literally being computed — but saying the distinction "disappears" invites future readers to fold them in unprincipled ways. Better wording: "Because we are operating purely structurally and not at constant pressure or volume, the distinction between Helmholtz- and Gibbs-style free energies does not bear on the algebra here. We use the analogy only as far as the energy-minus-entropy structure."

**(c) Appendix B sign error in Eqn. B-1.** As written:
$$
H = \sum_i \int dx_i\, q(x_i) \ln p(x_i, y_i\mid\theta).
$$
Numerical check in §12 Test 9: for $p(\eta) = (0.3, 0.7)$, $p(y=1\mid\eta) = (0.2, 0.9)$, uniform $q$:
- Maren's "$H$" as written = $-1.638$.
- Standard expected energy $\mathbb E_q[-\ln p] = +1.638$.
- These differ by a sign. The standard form is the one consistent with $F = $ energy $-$ entropy $\ge 0$ (in nats, for non-degenerate cases).

The fix is one character: insert a minus sign and call the symbol $U[q]$:
$$
U[q] = -\sum_i \int dx_i\, q(x_i) \ln p(x_i, y_i\mid\theta).
$$

## 11. CVM Computational Engine Audit

### 11.1 Positive assessment

Section 8 and Appendix C are the manuscript's most original contribution. The CVM provides an explicit internal free-energy landscape on a Markov-random-field grid with a Kikuchi cluster-entropy approximation that is more expressive than mean-field while remaining tractable. The configuration variables (single, NN-pair $y_i$, NNN-pair $w_i$, triplet $z_i$), degeneracy factors, and the analytic equilibrium expression Eqn. C-2 are coherent and traceable to Kikuchi & Brush (1967).

The motivating intuition — that representational units in active inference need an *internal* free-energy landscape to actually compute over, and that CVM provides one — is intellectually substantive.

### 11.2 Core mathematical problem

The manuscript does not show that the CVM free energy is the same functional as $F[q]$ in Eqn. 2, nor that it is a principled approximation of $F[q]$. The claim of "integration into the variational Bayes framework" rests entirely on:
- structural similarity (energy minus entropy form),
- shared use of the word "free energy,"
- the verbal assertion at [SOURCE A:578–582](1906.08804v6.pdf.txt:578) that "the model $q$ is formed by bringing the representational system $\tilde r$ into free energy equilibrium."

None of these constitute a derivation.

### 11.3 What is missing — a four-item checklist

To make Section 8 a derivation rather than a proposal, the manuscript must specify:

1. **Generative model** $p(y, \tilde\eta\mid m)$ in CVM-compatible form. What are the random variables? What is the joint? What does $m$ index?
2. **Variational family** $\mathcal Q$ of densities $q_\phi(\tilde\eta)$ parameterized by CVM configuration variables $\phi = (x_1, x_2, y_1, \dots, w_i, z_i)$ plus enthalpy parameters $(\epsilon_0, \epsilon_1)$. How do the CVM configurations parameterize the recognition density? Is this an exponential family? A maximum-entropy family with cluster constraints?
3. **Objective relationship.** Is $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(\phi) = F[q_\phi]$ exactly (theorem), or is $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(\phi) \ge F[q_\phi]$ (a surrogate upper bound), or is $|F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(\phi) - F[q_\phi]|$ controlled by some approximation parameter? Or is $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}$ a *separate* free-energy functional that has no formal relation to $F[q]$ but happens to be useful?
4. **Assumptions A1–A?** under which the bridge holds.

A revised proposition would look like:

> **Proposition (proposed; not yet proved).** Let $q_\phi(\tilde\eta)$ be the maximum-entropy density on the CVM grid with configuration-variable expectations $\phi$. Let $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(\phi)$ be the CVM free energy of Eqn. C-1. Under assumptions [to be stated]: $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(\phi) = F[q_\phi] + R(\phi)$ where the residual $R$ is bounded by [to be quantified].

### 11.4 Verdict

Section 8 is **promising but incomplete**. It should be repositioned as:
- a *proposal* for a recognition architecture,
- with an explicit research agenda to establish the four bridge items above,
- and clear language ("we propose," "we conjecture," "future work will establish") rather than claims of derivation.

This preserves the manuscript's originality without overclaiming. SOURCE C reaches the same conclusion (E10), and I confirm it independently.

### Test 5 — ELBO/VFE sign-convention trap

For uniform $q$ in Test 1's discrete model:
- $F_{\mathrm{Friston}} = 0.945$.
- $\mathrm{ELBO}_{\mathrm{Beal}} = -0.945$.
- $F + \mathrm{ELBO} = 0$. ✓
- Maren §6.2 says "$F$ is a lower bound for $L$" with $L = -\ln p$. Numerical: $F = 0.945$ vs. $L = 0.371$. So $F \le L$ would require $0.945 \le 0.371$, which is **false**. The standard $F \ge L$ holds. ✓

### Test 6 — L(s,a,r) expansion test

$N = 5$ iid Bernoulli draws from $p = 0.7$; sample = (0,1,1,1,0) (seed 42).
- Valid iid surprisal: $-\sum_{i=1}^5 \ln p(y_i) = 3.456$ nats.
- Maren-as-written, $-I \cdot \ln p(y) = -5\ln(0.69) = 1.855$ nats — assumes all $y_i = 1$.
- The two coincide only when all observations equal the same value. For any heterogeneous data, the literal Maren expansion is wrong.

If the intent was a *single* joint surprisal with summation suppressed inside, the LHS sum should be removed (this is the path Eqn. 17 takes). If the intent was iid factorization, it must be stated and the index $i$ must run over distinct data points.

### Test 7 — Measure transformation

$\eta \sim \mathcal N(0,1)$, $r = 2\eta + 3$ (a deterministic affine map). Compute $\mathbb E_\eta[\eta^2]$ vs. $\mathbb E_r[r^2]$:
- True $\mathbb E_\eta[\eta^2] = 0.996$ (sample, $N = 2\times 10^5$).
- Naive "treat $r$ as if it had the same role" $\mathbb E[r^2] = 13.024$.

The two differ by a non-trivial factor because no Jacobian / pushforward measure was applied. A correct change of variable: $\eta = (r-3)/2$, $d\eta = (1/2)\, dr$. Without that map, simply renaming the integration variable is invalid. Maren §3.2 / §5.4 propose exactly this rename without supplying the map.

### Test 8 — Markov blanket conditional independence

Construct a chain $\eta \to s \to \mu$, $\mu \to a$ (active state independent of $\eta$ given $\mu$). For all 4 settings of blanket $b = (s, a)$:
$$\max_{e,m} \bigl| p(\eta = e, \mu = m \mid b) - p(\eta = e \mid b)\, p(\mu = m \mid b) \bigr| < 6 \times 10^{-17}.$$

Conditional independence holds to machine precision. ✓ This is the *formal* Markov-blanket statement (SOURCE B Box 3.1). SOURCE A presents the *causal* / influence-arrow version but does not make the CI partition explicit.

### Test 9 — Thermodynamic analogy / Appendix B sign

For uniform $q$ in Test 1:
- Maren App B Eqn B-1 (as written): $\sum q \ln p(x,y) = -1.638$.
- Standard expected energy: $\mathbb E_q[-\ln p] = +1.638$.
- $F = $ standard energy $- H[q] = 1.638 - 0.693 = 0.945$ ✓
- $F$ via Maren's Eqn B-1 (no minus sign): $-1.638 - 0.693 = -2.331$ — wrong sign and wrong magnitude.

A single minus sign in Eqn B-1 fixes the discrepancy.

### Test 10 — CVM bridge test

The CVM Eqn. C-1 minimizes $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(x_1, y_i, w_i, z_i; \epsilon_1)$ over configuration variables on a 2-D grid. The active-inference $F[q]$ minimizes $\mathbb E_q[-\ln p] - H[q]$ over a recognition density $q$. These are two different variational principles applied to two different objects:
- $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}$: a Kikuchi cluster-variation approximation to the Gibbs free energy of an Ising-like Markov random field.
- $F[q]$: a divergence between an approximate posterior and a generative-model joint.

Identifying them requires (i) a generative model $p(y, \eta\mid m)$ that *is* the CVM Markov random field, (ii) a variational family $q_\phi$ parameterized by configuration variables, (iii) a proof that $F_{\mathrm{CVM}}(\phi) = F[q_\phi]$ or controlled approximation. None are supplied in SOURCE A.

The structural similarity is suggestive but not derivational.

## 15. Final Corrected Statement

This is the polished, publication-ready statement of the central mathematical content the manuscript can defensibly claim.

### 15.1 Safe theorem (the "publishable core")

> **Theorem (Variational free-energy identity).** Let $p(y, \tilde\eta\mid m)$ be a generative joint density over observed variables $y = (\tilde s, \tilde a, \tilde r)$ and hidden/external variables $\tilde\eta$, with $p(y\mid m) > 0$. Let $q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r)$ be a variational density absolutely continuous with respect to the model posterior $p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m) = p(y,\tilde\eta\mid m)/p(y\mid m)$, and assume $\ln p$ and $\ln q$ are $q$-integrable. Define the *variational free energy*
> $$F[q] := \mathbb E_q[-\ln p(y, \tilde\eta\mid m)] - H[q],$$
> where $H[q] = -\int q\ln q\, d\tilde\eta$ is the Shannon entropy. Then
> $$F[q] = D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r) \,\big\|\, p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)\bigr) - \ln p(y\mid m).$$
> Consequently, $F[q] \ge -\ln p(y\mid m)$, with equality iff $q(\tilde\eta\mid\tilde r) = p(\tilde\eta\mid y, m)$ a.e.

> **Corollary (Beal–Friston sign-convention bridge).** With the ELBO $\mathcal L(q) := \mathbb E_q[\ln p] + H[q]$, we have $F[q] = -\mathcal L(q)$. Therefore $\mathcal L(q) \le \ln p(y\mid m)$, and minimizing $F$ is equivalent to maximizing $\mathcal L$. Friston's variational free energy is the negative of Beal's variational lower bound, and the inequality direction flips accordingly.

This safe theorem is what SOURCE A is fundamentally about. It is publishable in this form. Everything else in the manuscript should be organized around establishing, interpreting, or extending it.

### 15.2 What the manuscript additionally establishes (with corrections applied)

- A useful *notation crosswalk* between Beal, Friston, and Blei (provided Table 4 is rebuilt per §7.3).
- A useful *pedagogical exposition* of how Friston's notation suppresses sums and integrals (provided the discipline of §7.4 is observed).
- A useful *thermodynamic analogy* (provided "enthalpy" is replaced by "expected energy" and the Helmholtz/Gibbs distinction is preserved as analogical, not collapsed).
- A useful *Markov-blanket framing* of the active-inference setting (provided the three meanings of "blanket" in §9.2 are separated and the model/process distinction is observed).

### 15.3 What the manuscript proposes but does not derive

- The CVM recognition architecture is a *proposal* of value. The four bridge items in §11.3 must be supplied to convert it into a derivation. Until then, language must say "proposed," "conjectured," "future work."
- The claim that external and internal systems each separately minimize free energy is a *modeling assumption* aligned with the NESS framing of Friston (2013). It is not implied by the variational identity alone.
